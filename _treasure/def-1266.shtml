Named Entity Recognition and An Apply on Document Clustering

When:October 18, 2004 @ 2:00 pm
Where:Dentistry 4111
Who:Wei, Gang
Title:Named Entity Recognition and An Apply on Document Clustering
Supervisor:Dr. E. Milios - Faculty of Computer Science
Readers:Dr. V. Keselj - Faculty of Computer Science
Dr. N. Zincir-Heywood - Faculty of Computer Science
Chair:Dr. R. Abidi - Faculty of Computer Science
Abstract:Named entity recognition is the task of processing a text and identifying the proper names. It has been studied aiming to promote research in Information Extraction and other Natural Language Processing applications. We create our own named entity recognition tool based on the maximum entropy principle. Maximum entropy is the statistics model widely used in the research of computational linguistics. We show that our system achieves good performance of named entity recognition, and it is a purely machine learning approach. The system works fast and is easy to build and port to other domains. The extracted named entities are used to define a document representation, which is tested on document clustering. Much research work on document clustering has been focused on the vector space model, where documents are represented by term vectors. We investigate whether the inclusion of named entities in the vector-space document representation helps to improve clustering performance. Our experiments show negative results in a small news wire document set: the named entities themselves are not suitable to be used as features in the document clustering process; named entities even slightly worsen the clustering results when they are used together with terms as features of document clustering. It appears that additional information needs to be used when named entities are made part of the document representation, such as co-reference resolution and use of context.
